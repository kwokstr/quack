The Big Dipper

The Big Dipper is a group of stars in the constellation Ursa Major. It looks like a mouse! The stars of the Big Dipper are the seven brightest stars of Ursa Major, making it especially noticeable. It also looks like a ladle or a cup with a long handle, like one of those big spoons that you cook soup with. It is considered the most recognizable star group in the Northern Hemisphere. In the constellation Ursa Major, which represents a big bear, the Big Dipper represents the tail. If you ever decide to go star-gazing in the Northern Hemisphere, you should definitely check out the Big Dipper! It is so fun.

ALKAID

Alkaid is loacted 210 light years from Earth. It is the last star of the tail of the Big Dipper, with a mass of about six Suns! When I searched up the meaning of Alkaid, it said it's "the leader of the daughters of the mourners."

DUBHE

Dubhe is the second brightest star in Ursa Major, and is one of the brightest stars in the sky. It is 123 light-years from Earth, representing the top right corner of the bowl of the Big Dipper.

Luminosity : 316L⊙

MEGREZ

Megrez is considered the faintest star in the Big Dipper. But you know what, that's a-okay! It is about 58 lights years from Earth. Megrez is still young, being 300 million years old.

Luminosity : 14L⊙
